San Jose City Parks Assessment

Turf Appearance

Turf Appearance

Rating Meaning

1 - Unacceptable
75 % or more weeds, bare spots or brown patches. May include extensive gopher/ squirrel activity. Gopher/squirrel program abatement necessary.

Recommended Action
Fence off Park. Schedule complete turf renovation.

2 - Needs Improvement
50% or more weeds, bare spots, brown patches. May include extensive gopher/squirrel activiity.

Recommended Action
Fence off portion of the park. Renovate up to 50% of turf.

3 - Acceptable
25% - 50% weeds, bare spots, brown patches. May include some gopher/ squirrel activity. Establish maint. for vertebrate abatement.

Recommended Action
Good condition overall. Playable. Monitor monthly gopher/ squirrel activity. Spot treat for broadleaf. Overseed.

4 - Very Good
10% - 25% weeds, bare spots, brown patches. May include slight gopher/ squirrel activity.

Recommended Action
Very good condition. Turf is healthy. Monitor gophers/ squirrels. Trap as necessary. Aerate/fertilize 2x's/yr.

5 - New
10 %- or fewer weeds, bare spots, brown patches. No gopher/ squirrel activity.

Recommended Action
None. Brand New Facility.

Sports Fields

Sports Fields

Playability

3 - Standard
Neighborhood parks with heavy traffic; limited resources resulting in low turf management intensity causing moderate to heavy infestation of weeds.
Practice fields (pick-up games) may show signficant wear/ damage. Average of 10 hours of soccer play and/ or 12 hours of baseball/ week.

4 - Choice
Free of bumpiness, weed clumps, mounds, slopes on the grade, wet and dry spots, bare areas, and holes or other obstructions.
Moderate to high traffic. Management intensity is high. These fields support leagues. Average of 18 hours of scheduled soccer play and/ or 20 hours of baseball/ week.

5 - Premium
Professional sports grade.
High attention to detail. Manicured, borders trimmed, big and little holes filled; lines are straight, mowing patterns are attractive.

Rating Meaning

1 - Unacceptable
Play area is unsafe. Extensive gopher/ squirrel activity causing open holes in turf. Backstops, dugouts and bleachers unsafe/ need replacement. Turf is 75% or more of weeds.

Recommended Action
Turf is not playable. Close field for repairs. Needs major renovation.

2 - Needs Improvement
Turf area consists of 50% or more weeds. Potential tripping hazards from woody weeds (mallow), worn areas, or from gopher/squirrel/ mole activity.

Recommended Action
Schedule renovation and close off area(s) deemed unsafe.

3 - Acceptable
The turf is in good condition. It has between 25-50% weeds. It may include brown or worn spots. A partial field renovation may be necessary.

Recommended Action
Schedule renovation and /or rest field.

4 - Very Good
Turf is mowed, green with very few weeds. Generally healthy. Highly recommend a horticultural turf crew. Must renovate 2-4 x/s per year.

Recommended Action
Monitor condition of field. In general, it's in very good playable condition.

5 - New
Turf, bleachers, backstops, and dug-outs are new. No gopher/squirrel activity. Fertilized and the sprinklers are flush with the surface.

Recommended Action
None. Newly renovated Sports Field.

Trees

Trees

Rating Meaning

1 - Unacceptable
75% or more of the tree appears to be dead. May be considered unsafe; could be a potential fire hazrd.

Recommended Action
Schedule Removal

2 - Needs Improvement
Showing early signs of decline; discolored leaves, or dead branches throughout canopy.

Recommended Action
Schedule major structural pruning. Talk to arborist about tree injection. Check for soil compaction, potential disease. Deep root irrigate and feed.

3 - Acceptable
Good strong canopy; may need corrective pruning or require thinning.

Recommended Action
Schedule pruning and thinning as needed. Deep root fertilize/irrigate.

4 - Very Good
Appears healthy; good green color, no disease. May require structural or minor pruning.

Recommended Action
Monitor condition and make notes of obvious changes.

5 - New
Newly Planted

Recommended Action
No action necessary.

Tree Basins

Tree Basins

Rating Meaning

1 - Unacceptable
Groundcover, (g.c.) growing up into the tree. Weeds taken over 75 % or more of the tree basin. Unhealthy for tree.

Recommended Action
Unacceptable. Remove g.c. and weeds. Add mulch to basin for weed control.

2 - Needs Improvement
Tree basin has 50% OR MORE weeds. Needs improvement.

Recommended Action
Needs improvement. Remove weeds and add mulch. (leaf debris).

3 - Acceptable
Basin has 25-50 % weeds

Recommended Action
Acceptable condition. Schedule weed abatement and mulch application for near future.

4 - Very Good
Weeds are 10-25 % per basin/average.

Recommended Action
Very good condition. Monitor.

5 - New
Less than 10% weeds in tree basins.

Recommended Action
No action necessary. Maintenance performed recently.
